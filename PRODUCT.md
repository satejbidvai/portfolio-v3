# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Frontend engineers, hiring managers, and tech-curious readers who land here from dev.to reposts, social links, or search. They're scanning for signal: can this person write, think, and build with care? Most visit on desktop, mid-workday, skimming before deciding whether to read a full post or reach out.

## Product Purpose

A personal brand surface for Satej Bidvai, frontend developer. Not a portfolio of shipped projects; a demonstration of taste and thinking through writing. The site itself is the proof: if the typography, spacing, and transitions feel right before you've read a word, the message has already landed. Success is someone finishing a blog post and thinking "this person cares about the craft."

## Brand Personality

Precise, understated, self-aware. Confident enough to let the work speak without decoration. Witty without trying hard (the OCD joke lands because it's one line, not a bit). The Teller quote is the manifesto: magic is just unreasonable effort made invisible.

## Anti-references

- Developer portfolios with particle effects, 3D globes, or scroll-hijacking animations. Spectacle that says "look at me" instead of "read this."
- Template portfolios: colorful hero, icon-grid skills section, testimonial carousel, contact form. The Vercel/Next.js starter-portfolio genre.
- Corporate SaaS landing pages with gradient meshes and "Trusted by" logo bars.
- Dribbble-bait: glassmorphism, neon-on-dark, stacked card grids with identical structure.
- Blogs that look like documentation sites. MDX is for writing, not for API refs.

## Design Principles

1. **Effort should be invisible.** The font metrics fallback, the word-joiner entities in the OCD line, the View Transitions circle animation on theme toggle: care is embedded in details nobody will consciously notice. That's the point.
2. **Show taste through restraint.** What's absent (accent colors, illustrations, project cards, animations) says more than what's present. Every addition must clear a high bar.
3. **Let the writing speak.** The site is a container for prose. Chrome should disappear; reading comfort is the primary design goal.
4. **Precision is personality.** The personality comes through in typographic choices, spacing decisions, and interaction details, not through decorative elements or clever copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Reduced-motion support already implemented (fade-in animations and theme toggle respect `prefers-reduced-motion`). Skip links present. Screen-reader-only headings for landmark structure. Color contrast must hold in both light and dark themes across all text hierarchy levels.
